Mozart in a nutshell

The Mozart Programming System combines research in programming language design and implementation, constraint logic programming, distributed computing, and human-computer interfaces.

Mozart provides expressive power and advanced functionality. Its 64-bit implementation supports modern architectures and provides both compiled and REPL interpreter execution for incremental development and experimentation.

If you're interested in languages or looking for a new platform to support distributed computing research we're confident you will find Mozart has something to offer.

Distribution

Distribution is pervasive in modern ICT systems. Mozart has achieved novel results in open and transparent distribution. It is used in ongoing research in Distributed Systems and Algorithms, supporting such accomplishments as a distributed unification algorithm, the Selfman peer-to-peer project, and CRDT (conflict-free replicated data-type) research.

Multiparadigm

Mozart builds many commonly known but typically disparate programming models upon a simple procedural kernel with a well-defined and complete semantics. It includes procedural, functional, object, logic and constraint programming models, which may coexist in Oz programs, enabling its users to select the most suitable paradigm for a task.
